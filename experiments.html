  • 1. Study of Compound Microscope Learn the parts, focusing techniques, and safety of using a compound microscope.
  • 2. Identification of Axial Bones Identify and understand bones forming the axial skeleton: skull, vertebral column, rib cage.
  • 3. Identification of Appendicular Bones Study bones of the limbs and girdles forming the appendicular skeleton.
  • 4. Study of Permanent Slides of Muscle Tissues Observe and differentiate skeletal, cardiac, and smooth muscle tissues under the microscope.
  • 5. Study of Permanent Slides of Nervous Tissue Examine neurons and glial cells, and their structure.
  • 6. Study of Permanent Slides of Epithelial Tissue Study types and characteristics of epithelial tissues.
  • 7. Study of Permanent Slides of Connective Tissue Identify and compare bone, cartilage, blood, and other connective tissues.
  • 8. Study of Human Heart Model Explore the structure and function of the heart using models.
  • 9. Study of Human Brain Model Understand parts and functions of the brain through models.
  • 10. Study of Human Eye Model Investigate the anatomy of the human eye using models.
  • 11. Study of Human Ear Model Study the structure of the ear and its role in hearing and balance.
  • 12. Study of Human Kidney Model Learn about kidney structure and its role in filtration and urine formation.
  • 13. Study of Human Liver Model Understand liver structure and its metabolic functions.
  • 14. Study of Human Lungs Model Study lung structure and the process of respiration.
  • 15. Study of Human Skin Model Examine layers and functions of the human skin.
